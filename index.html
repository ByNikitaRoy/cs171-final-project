How has the global media covered the war in Ukraine?

On February 24, 2022 Russia launched a full scale invasion of Ukraine, initiating the largest military mobilization in Europe since World War II. The role of the media in times of war can be very influential in either reinforcing or changing public opinion. Our team embarked on a study to understand and analyze the global media coverage of this war.

WHAT CAN WE LEARN FROM ANALYZING GLOBAL MEDIA?

Our analysis of the global media coverage of this war deconstructs regional biases and trends that influenced the news coverage. Through the following interactive visualizations, we unpack online news and broadcast media coverage of this war from various geographical locations worldwide.
This study sourced data from the GDELT project, which “monitors the world's news media from nearly every corner of every country in print, broadcast, and web formats, in over 100 languages, every moment of every day.”

BRINGING IT BACK TO WHAT IT MATTERS

We have seen how news coverage varies across media outlets, countries and time. These patterns inevitably affect our perceptions of the “truth.” What we read on the news was someone's choice: it’s a product of personal opinion, geopolitical context, and potentially so much more. The news doesn’t necessarily reflect the priorities of victims on the ground.

A HUMAN CONFLICT

Sources of news are ultimately set up to inform the public about current events, and in the translation of facts to articles, journalists imbue content with opinions informed by national priorities. Our access to and analysis of big data has helped us begin to understand what facts are communicated, and more importantly how they are communicated.

ABOUT THE PROJECT

THE GDELT DATABASE

The Global Database of Events, Language and Tone is a big data project led by Kalev Leetaru. It collects news reports about events around the world and parses the news reports for actors, events, and geography with the aim of providing a comprehensive set of data for researchers, governments, and civic society.
