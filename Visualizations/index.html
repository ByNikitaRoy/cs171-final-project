A media and language analysis of the conflict.

On February 24, 2022 Russia launched a full scale invasion of Ukraine, initiating the largest military mobilization in Europe since World War II. The effects of the invasion have been felt around the world and media coverage has been ubiquitous.

WHAT CAN WE LEARN FROM ANALYZING GLOBAL MEDIA?

As you read through the interactive data visualizations in this article, you will be able to explore how web and television media is covering the Russia - Ukraine conflict. By deconstructing patterns and trends in the coverage of the Russia-Ukraine conflict we can expose regional biases, interests, and priorities.
This project leverages data from the GDELT project, which monitors the world's news media in print, broadcast, and web formats, in over 100 languages, every moment of every day.

BRINGING IT BACK TO WHAT IT MATTERS

We have seen how news coverage varies across media outlets, countries and time. These patterns inevitably affect our perceptions of the “truth.” What we read on the news was someone's choice: it’s a product of personal opinion, geopolitical context, and potentially so much more. The news doesn’t necessarily reflect the priorities of victims on the ground.

A HUMAN CONFLICT

This case study deconstructs the nature of news coverage around the world, as it pertains to a modern armed conflict. It’s evident that there is always going to be a gap between what gets covered in the media and the day-to-day reality of victims.

However, sources of news are ultimately set up to inform the public about current events. And in the translation of facts to articles, journalists imbue content with opinions informed by national priorities. Our access to and analysis of big data has helped us begin to understand what facts are communicated, and more importantly how they are communicated.

ABOUT THE PROJECT

THE GDELT DATABASE

The Global Database of Events, Language and Tone is a big data project led by Kalev Leetaru. It collects news reports about events around the world and parses the news reports for actors, events, and geography with the aim of providing a comprehensive set of data for researchers, governments, and civic society.
